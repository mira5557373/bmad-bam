### Availability Exclusions

The following are excluded from availability calculations:
- Scheduled maintenance windows (with proper notice)
- Force majeure events
- Third-party service outages (cloud provider, payment processor)
- Customer-initiated service disruptions
- Beta or preview features

## Compensation Terms

<!-- Service credits for SLA breaches. -->

### Availability Credit Schedule

| Monthly Uptime         | Service Credit (% of Monthly Fee) |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| < Target, >= Target-1% | 10%                               |
| < Target-1%, >= Target-2% | 25%                            |
| < Target-2%, >= Target-5% | 50%                            |
| < Target-5%            | 100%                              |

### Credit Limitations

- Maximum credit per month: 100% of monthly fee
- Credits do not carry over to subsequent months
- Credits are non-refundable, non-transferable
- Customer must request credits within 30 days of incident
- Credits apply to base subscription only, not usage fees

### Credit Request Process

| Step | Action                                    | Timeline        |
| ---- | ----------------------------------------- | --------------- |
| 1    | Customer submits credit request via portal | Within 30 days  |
| 2    | Platform validates SLA breach             | 5 business days |
| 3    | Credit determination notification         | 3 business days |
| 4    | Credit applied to next invoice            | Next billing cycle |

## Terms and Conditions

### Effective Period

This SLA is effective from {{effective_date}} and remains in force for the duration of the subscription agreement unless superseded by an updated SLA document.

### Modification

Platform reserves the right to modify this SLA with 30 days notice. Material changes that reduce service commitments require customer consent for Enterprise tier.

### Governing Provisions

In case of conflict between this SLA and the Master Service Agreement, the Master Service Agreement prevails unless the SLA provides more favorable terms to the customer.
