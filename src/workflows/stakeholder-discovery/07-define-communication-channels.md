# Step 07: Define Communication Channels

## Purpose

Establish effective communication paths, channels, and cadences for each stakeholder group based on their priority tier, preferences, and information needs. This ensures stakeholders receive appropriate information through their preferred channels at the right frequency to maintain engagement and support.

## Prerequisites

- [ ] Steps 01-06 are complete
- [ ] Engagement priority tiers are defined
- [ ] Stakeholder disposition is known
- [ ] Understanding of available communication tools and platforms
- [ ] Knowledge of organizational communication norms

## Actions

1. **Inventory Available Communication Channels**
   - Document available channels by category:
     - **Synchronous:** Meetings (in-person, video), phone calls, instant messaging
     - **Asynchronous:** Email, project portals, documentation wikis, recorded videos
     - **Broadcast:** Newsletters, announcements, all-hands meetings, dashboards
     - **Interactive:** Workshops, feedback sessions, surveys, office hours
     - **Formal:** Steering committees, review boards, governance meetings
   - Note channel availability by tenant type/organization

2. **Map Channel Preferences by Stakeholder Type**
   - Assess channel effectiveness for each stakeholder category:
     - **Executives:** Brief updates, dashboards, steering committee meetings
     - **Technical Stakeholders:** Technical documentation, architecture reviews, demos
     - **Compliance Stakeholders:** Formal documentation, audit trails, review meetings
     - **End Users:** Training sessions, user guides, feedback forums
   - Document cultural and organizational preferences

3. **Define Communication Cadence by Priority Tier**
   - Establish frequency guidelines:
     - **Tier 1 (Critical):** Weekly touchpoints, immediate escalation access
     - **Tier 2 (Important):** Bi-weekly updates, monthly reviews
     - **Tier 3 (Standard):** Monthly updates, quarterly reviews
     - **Tier 4 (Minimal):** Quarterly newsletters, major milestone announcements
   - Allow flexibility based on project phase and stakeholder needs

4. **Design Communication Templates**
   - Create standardized templates for:
     - Executive status updates (brief, outcome-focused)
     - Technical progress reports (detailed, metric-driven)
     - Compliance status reports (evidence-based, risk-focused)
     - User communications (clear, action-oriented)
   - Ensure templates align with stakeholder information needs

5. **Establish Escalation Communication Paths**
   - Define escalation triggers and channels:
     - Risk identification: Who to notify, how quickly
     - Blocker encountered: Escalation chain and response times
     - Major milestone: Celebration and acknowledgment channels
     - Stakeholder concern: Issue resolution path
   - Document escalation protocols for each tier

6. **Map Multi-Tenant Communication Considerations**
   - Address tenant-specific communication needs:
     - **Enterprise:** Formal governance structures, executive briefings
     - **SMB:** Efficient, consolidated communications
     - **Government:** Compliance-aware, documented communications
   - Identify cross-tenant communication requirements
   - Document confidentiality boundaries between tenants

7. **Define Feedback Collection Mechanisms**
   - Establish channels for stakeholder input:
     - Surveys (periodic and event-triggered)
     - Feedback forms (embedded in communications)
     - Office hours (regular availability for questions)
     - Advisory groups (structured input forums)
   - Map feedback channels to stakeholder types

8. **Create Communication Calendar Framework**
   - Develop recurring communication schedule:
     - Weekly: Tier 1 stakeholder touchpoints
     - Bi-weekly: Tier 2 updates
     - Monthly: Broader stakeholder updates, newsletter
     - Quarterly: All-stakeholder reviews, retrospectives
   - Identify key milestone communication events

9. **Assign Communication Responsibilities**
   - Define roles for communication management:
     - Overall stakeholder communication owner
     - Tier-specific communication leads
     - Technical communication contributors
     - Executive communication handlers
   - Document RACI for communication activities

## Verification

- [ ] All communication channels are inventoried and assessed
- [ ] Each stakeholder tier has defined channels and cadence
- [ ] Communication templates are created for key stakeholder types
- [ ] Escalation paths are documented and understood
- [ ] Feedback mechanisms are established
- [ ] Communication responsibilities are assigned
