# Step 02: Analyze Decision Makers

## Purpose

Map the technical and business decision makers within each stakeholder group to understand authority structures, approval chains, and influence hierarchies. This analysis enables targeted engagement strategies and ensures critical decision makers are appropriately prioritized throughout the project lifecycle.

## Prerequisites

- [ ] Step 01 (Identify Tenant Stakeholders) is complete
- [ ] Tenant Stakeholder Matrix is available
- [ ] Initial Stakeholder Inventory has been created
- [ ] Access to organizational charts or reporting structures (where available)
- [ ] Understanding of typical enterprise decision-making patterns

## Actions

1. **Categorize Decision-Making Authority**
   - Define authority levels: Final Approval, Recommendation Authority, Veto Power, Advisory Only
   - Create decision domain categories: Technical Architecture, Budget/Procurement, Security/Compliance, Operations
   - Document escalation paths for each decision domain

2. **Map Technical Decision Makers**
   - Identify technical authority holders for each tenant type:
     - Architecture decisions (platform integration, API design)
     - Technology stack selections (AI/ML frameworks, infrastructure)
     - Security and compliance technical requirements
     - Performance and scalability requirements
   - Document their decision scope and constraints

3. **Map Business Decision Makers**
   - Identify business authority holders:
     - Budget holders and financial approvers
     - Strategic initiative sponsors
     - Business requirement owners
     - Contract and procurement authorities
   - Understand their success metrics and priorities

4. **Analyze Decision-Making Patterns**
   - Document typical decision timelines by tenant type
   - Identify consensus vs. hierarchical decision cultures
   - Map informal influencers who affect formal decisions
   - Note any decision-making bottlenecks or blockers

5. **Create Decision Authority Matrix**
   - Build RACI-style matrix for key decision types:
     - R (Responsible): Who executes
     - A (Accountable): Who has final authority
     - C (Consulted): Who provides input
     - I (Informed): Who needs to know
   - Validate matrix against real-world decision examples

6. **Identify Decision Maker Dependencies**
   - Map relationships between decision makers
   - Document approval chain sequences
   - Identify parallel vs. sequential approval requirements
   - Note any external dependencies (board approval, regulatory review)

7. **Document Decision Maker Profiles**
   - Create brief profiles for key decision makers:
     - Decision domains and authority level
     - Known priorities and concerns
     - Preferred communication style
     - Historical decision patterns (if known)

## Verification

- [ ] All major decision domains have identified authority holders
- [ ] Decision Authority Matrix covers technical and business decisions
- [ ] Approval chains are documented for critical decision types
- [ ] Key decision maker profiles are complete
- [ ] Decision-making patterns are documented by tenant type

## Outputs

| Output | Description | Format |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Decision Authority Matrix | RACI-style matrix for key decisions | Spreadsheet |
| Decision Maker Profiles | Individual profiles of key decision makers | Document |
| Approval Chain Diagrams | Visual representation of approval sequences | Diagram |
| Decision Pattern Analysis | Summary of decision-making cultures by tenant | Document |
