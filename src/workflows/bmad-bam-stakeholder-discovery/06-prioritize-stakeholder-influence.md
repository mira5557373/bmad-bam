# Step 06: Prioritize Stakeholder Influence

## Purpose

Create a comprehensive influence/interest matrix to prioritize stakeholder engagement efforts. This prioritization ensures limited project resources are focused on stakeholders with the greatest impact on project success while maintaining appropriate relationships with all identified stakeholders.

## Prerequisites

- [ ] Steps 01-05 are complete
- [ ] Comprehensive stakeholder inventory is compiled
- [ ] Decision maker analysis is available
- [ ] Understanding of project goals and success criteria
- [ ] Knowledge of organizational politics and dynamics (where available)

## Actions

1. **Define Influence Assessment Criteria**
   - Establish influence dimensions:
     - **Decision Authority:** Ability to approve, reject, or modify project scope
     - **Resource Control:** Control over budget, personnel, or technical resources
     - **Political Capital:** Organizational influence beyond formal authority
     - **Technical Expertise:** Subject matter expertise affecting credibility
     - **Relationship Networks:** Connections to other key stakeholders
   - Create scoring rubric (1-5 scale) for each dimension

2. **Define Interest Assessment Criteria**
   - Establish interest dimensions:
     - **Direct Impact:** How much the project affects their role/team
     - **Strategic Alignment:** Connection to their goals and priorities
     - **Risk Exposure:** Potential negative impacts to their domain
     - **Opportunity Perception:** Potential benefits they may gain
     - **Historical Engagement:** Past involvement in similar initiatives
   - Create scoring rubric (1-5 scale) for each dimension

3. **Score All Identified Stakeholders**
   - Apply influence criteria to each stakeholder
   - Apply interest criteria to each stakeholder
   - Calculate composite influence and interest scores
   - Document rationale for scoring decisions
   - Flag stakeholders with uncertain scores for validation

4. **Create Influence/Interest Matrix**
   - Plot stakeholders on 2x2 matrix:
     - **High Influence/High Interest (Manage Closely):** Key players requiring active engagement
     - **High Influence/Low Interest (Keep Satisfied):** Powerful stakeholders to keep informed
     - **Low Influence/High Interest (Keep Informed):** Supportive stakeholders to engage regularly
     - **Low Influence/Low Interest (Monitor):** Stakeholders requiring minimal attention
   - Identify stakeholders near quadrant boundaries

5. **Analyze Stakeholder Clusters**
   - Identify natural groupings of stakeholders:
     - By organizational unit
     - By stakeholder type (decision maker, consumer, compliance, integration)
     - By tenant type
     - By influence/interest quadrant
   - Document cluster dynamics and relationships

6. **Assess Stakeholder Disposition**
   - Categorize stakeholder attitude toward project:
     - **Champions:** Active supporters who will advocate
     - **Supporters:** Positive but passive
     - **Neutral:** No strong opinion either way
     - **Skeptics:** Have concerns but open to persuasion
     - **Blockers:** Actively opposed or resistant
   - Map disposition to influence/interest position

7. **Identify Coalition Opportunities**
   - Identify potential stakeholder alliances:
     - Natural allies who share interests
     - Complementary stakeholders (technical + business)
     - Influential champions who can sway others
   - Document coalition building strategies

8. **Create Engagement Priority Tiers**
   - Tier 1 (Critical): High influence stakeholders requiring intensive engagement
   - Tier 2 (Important): Significant stakeholders requiring regular engagement
   - Tier 3 (Standard): Stakeholders requiring periodic engagement
   - Tier 4 (Minimal): Stakeholders requiring only broadcast communications
   - Assign stakeholders to tiers with engagement frequency guidelines

9. **Document Priority Rationale**
   - For each high-priority stakeholder, document:
     - Why they are critical to project success
     - Key risks if not properly engaged
     - Specific engagement objectives
     - Success metrics for engagement

## Verification

- [ ] All stakeholders have influence and interest scores
- [ ] Influence/Interest matrix is complete and visualized
- [ ] Stakeholder disposition is assessed for all stakeholders
- [ ] Engagement priority tiers are defined with stakeholder assignments
- [ ] Coalition opportunities are identified
- [ ] Priority rationale is documented for Tier 1 stakeholders
